# Reproducible Research

The reproducible research program is the route into workflow discipline,
build systems, automation, and scientific execution habits. It shows how
engineering structure can be taught through practical research tooling
without reducing the work to tool-specific recipes.

## Why It Matters

This family is not just about research tooling. It is about engineering
judgment under workflow pressure: build-graph truth, publish boundaries,
dynamic workflow safety, state identity, and reproducible execution.
The programs are organized by failure mode and design pressure rather
than by tool popularity.

## What It Makes Visible

- workflow and automation thinking expressed as a teachable system
- comfort with build, workflow, and reproducibility tooling that matters in real technical and scientific environments
- capstone-backed programs where the claims stay attached to runnable proof
- the ability to teach system models instead of memorizing command syntax
